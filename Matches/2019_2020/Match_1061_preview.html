    Team News

    Real Madrid
    • Gareth Bale helped Real Madrid to victory over Real Sociedad from the bench on Saturday but his arrival to the pitch was met by boos and whistles.
    • The Wales international will hope to start such an important game like this one but it remains to be seen whether that will be the case.
    • Toni Kroos will return to the side after dropping to the bench at the weekend.
    • Lucas Vazquez and James Rodriguez both remain sidelined for Zinedine Zidane's side, while Nacho is a doubt.
    Paris Saint-Germain
    • Neymar returned from injury at the weekend but is reportedly only going to make the bench against Real Madrid.
    • It means Paris Saint-Germain supporters will have to wait to see Neymar and Kylian Mbappe start their first game together this season.
    • Marco Verratti has made the travelling squad for Tuesday's game after missing out at the weekend through injury, but Ander Herrera, Layvin Kurzawa, Eric Maxim Choupo-Moting and Thilo Kehrer remain sidelined.
    • Di Maria and Verratti will miss PSG's final group game if they are booked against Madrid.

    Prediction

    • In order to stay in the hunt for top spot in Group A, Real Madrid have to beat leaders Paris Saint-Germain on Tuesday. Zinedine Zidane's side trail PSG by five points heading into the last two matches.
    • PSG will secure top spot in the group by avoiding defeat in midweek. Thomas Tuchel's side beat Madrid 3-0 in the reverse fixture back in September and that remains their standout performance of 2019/20.
    • Madrid are unbeaten at home in all competitions this season (W6, D3), while PSG have won seven of their last eight away from home. This is a difficult match to call, as both teams have been difficult to read throughout the campaign. Home soil and history would favour Madrid but PSG defeated Madrid in emphatic fashion earlier this season.
