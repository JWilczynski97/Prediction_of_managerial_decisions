    Team News

    Barcelona
    Napoli
    • Barcelona are terribly light in midfield with both Arturo Vidal and Sergio Busquets banned. Frenkie de Jong will move into his favoured central midfield position and will reportedly line up with Ivan Rakitic and Sergi Roberto.
    • Jordi Alba and Luis Suarez have recovered since the original date of this Champions League round of 16 second leg tie and will return to the starting XI. Samuel Umtiti and Ousmane Dembele are major doubts.
    • Antoine Griezmann, Lionel Messi and Nelson Semedo will miss Barcelona's next game in the Champions League if they are booked here.
    • Ansu Fati and Griezmann are in competition for a place alongside Suarez and Messi up front.
    • Kostas Manolas recovered from injury to start Napoli's 3-1 win over Lazio at the weekend, timely given Nikola Maksimovic is expected to miss out.
    • Napoli have confirmed Lorenzo Insigne is a major doubt to face Barcelona with a thigh injury and his absence would be a big blow for the away side.
    • 20-year-old Eljif Elmas has been tipped to replace Insigne if he fails to recover from injury in time over the likes of Hirving Lozano or Matteo Politano.
    • Diego Demme and Stanislav Lobotka are in competition for a place in central midfield for the away side, with Allan further down the pecking order.
    • Kalidou Koulibaly, Fernando Llorente and Mario Rui will incur a one-match ban in the Champions League if they are booked here.
    • Signed for the 2020/21 season, Victor Osimhen is not eligible to play for Napoli here.

    Prediction

    • Barcelona are favourites on paper to advance after managing a 1-1 draw away to Napoli back in March but nothing is straightforward for the Spanish side at the moment. They limped across the line in LaLiga and now have to contend with key players missing here.
    • Napoli also struggled for consistency towards the end of the season in Serie A but did round off their league campaign with a 3-1 win over high flying Lazio. Napoli are impressively unbeaten in the Champions League this season and held Liverpool to a 1-1 draw at Anfield in the group stage. They have a genuine chance to eliminate Barcelona on Saturday.
