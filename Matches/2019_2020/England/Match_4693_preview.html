    Team News

    Man Utd
    Liverpool
    • Manchester United manager Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has confirmed he hopes to welcome back Aaron Wan-Bissaka and Anthony Martial for Sunday's welcome of rivals Liverpool. Luke Shaw will also require late fitness test, with Ashley Young or Axel Tuanzebe ready to deputise at left-back.
    • However, Solskjaer has confirmed David de Gea and Paul Pogba are both ruled. The injury De Gea picked up on international duty is not as bad as first feared and he should return before the next international break, but Pogba has failed to recover in time from a foot injury sustained before the break.
    • Victor Lindelof has recovered from a back injury and will hope to regain his starting berth at centre-back for the home side.
    • Daniel James suffered a head injury for Wales during the international break and may need assessment from United's medical staff ahead of Sunday's match.
    • Phil Jones, Jesse Lingard, Eric Bailly, Diogo Dalot and Timothy Fosu-Mensah are other injury concerns for Solskjaer.
    • The big news for Liverpool is that Alisson is ready to make his comeback after missing Liverpool's last 11 games through injury. Despite Adrian's good performances, Alisson is expected straight back in the starting XI here.
    • Joel Matip is also back in full training and should be fine to start at Old Trafford, while Trent Alexander-Arnold was an unused substitute in both of England's matches during the break and will be fresh here.
    • Mohamed Salah escaped serious injury in Liverpool's win over Leicester before the international break but is still rated as a doubt heading into Sunday's match.
    • Harvey Elliott has been suspended from all domestic club football for 14 days after admitting derogatory language about Harry Kane in a video from June. He wouldn't have featured here anyway.

    Prediction

    • Manchester United are already 15 points behind Liverpool after only eight league games played this season.
    • Ole Gunnar Solskjaer's side have scored more than once in a game in just three of their last 23 matches and that simply won't be good enough to get something from this match.

    • As Liverpool prepare to welcome back their No.1 goalkeeper, United could be without their's on Sunday. David de Gea pulled up with a muscle injury during the international break and his absence is a huge blow for the home side.
    • United are surprisingly unbeaten in their last six home matches against Liverpool (W3, D3) but are not expected to get even a point on Sunday.
