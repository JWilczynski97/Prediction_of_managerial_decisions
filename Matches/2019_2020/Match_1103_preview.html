    Team News

    PSG
    Borussia Dortmund
    • Kylian Mbappe is included in the Paris Saint-Germain squad despite coming down with a fever and sore throat.
    • Despite being in the squad, it's still unclear whether Mbappe will be fit enough to start. We could see Pablo Sarabia and Edinson Cavani start up front.
    • Thomas Tuchel must do without Marco Verratti and Thomas Meunier, as both are suspended for Wednesday night.
    • Thiago Silva is also missing for the home side after failing to recover from injury.
    • With Verratti missing, Tuchel will be desperate to keep Marquinhos in central midfield but starting one of Abdou Diallo or 17-year-old Tanguy Kouassi might be too much of a gamble.
    • With Colin Dagba missing, Thilo Kehrer is expected to start at right-back on Wednesday night.
    • Lucien Favre is unable to call upon injured duo Marco Reus and Thomas Delaney for Dortmund's second leg clash with PSG.
    • He is unlikely to make wholesale changes to his favoured XI and may in fact name the same side that started the first leg 2-1 win over the French side.

    Prediction

    • PSG bagged a crucial away goal in Germany as they seek to avoid another last-16 Champions League exit. The French side have had extra time to prepare for this clash after their league meeting with Strasbourg was postponed due to Coronavirus at the weekend.
    • For Borussia Dortmund, they come into the tie in Paris on a five-match winning streak and hold a slender advantage in their bid to bag a quarter-final spot. They have, however, been very inconsistent on the road of late having won three and lost three of their last six away matches, which doesn't bode well against a PSG side that has failed to win just one of their last 13 at home.
    • Goals are expected to be the order of the day in Paris in what sure to be another entertaining encounter.
