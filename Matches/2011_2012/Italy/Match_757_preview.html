    Prediction

    • Inter have no time to lose or to drop points. Winning this match is mandatory, and despite owner Massimo Moratti saying there is no chance of a managerial change before the end of the season, Ranieri's job could be at risk if he doesn't manage to beat Novara. He is going to try again with the 4-3-1-2, leaving Alvarez out and relying on his most experienced man rather than the solid 4-4-2 full of youngsters that brought Inter so far up the table after their dreadful start to the season.
    • Five defeats in a row, and then a draw at home against Cagliari: Novara are even more desperate at the moment, and coach Mondonico also has a very hard clash at the San Siro to contend with. When the two teams met in September, Novara unexpectedly beat Inter at home, but since then they only managed to win once.
    • Expect Inter to press Novara, trying to prevent them the chance to counter-attack. Ranieri will try to take the lead early and close the deal in the first half, but Mondonico will use all of his experience to take countermeasures and prevent the Neroazzurri. As the Inter coach has some players back from injury, more competition will be generated amongst team mates, and in the end some stars that are not performing so well may come back to shine again.
