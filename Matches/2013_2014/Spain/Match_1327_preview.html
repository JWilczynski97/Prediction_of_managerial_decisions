    Prediction

    • The game everyone is waiting for. Arguably the most anticipated domestic match-up in the world sees Spain's big 'two' come head-to-head. With four points separating league-leaders Madrid and Barcelona, this is a must win game for the Catalans.
    • The recent history is against Barcelona for this fixture. They haven't beaten the capital side on their home ground since 2012, but Tata Martino's men will be encouraged by the previous meeting between the two in October, where they won comfortably.
    • Interestingly, Neymar returns to the starting line-up for Barcelona. Many believe that the Brazilian has not been at his best lately and that Lionel Messi plays much better when the Brazilian is absent. Last weekend's rout of Osasuna was evidence of this but Tata Martino will find it hard to keep Neymar on the bench again. Aside from Neymar's inclusion, it is the same side that demolished the Pamplonans.
    • Much will depend on Lionel Messi. This season Barca have not been at their vintage best, but when Messi has been on song, so to have Barca. Messi revels in playing against Los Blancos and should he get two goals he will attain the highest number of goals of any player in the history of El Clasico.
    • While Barca ran out winners in this fixture earlier in the season, Madrid are a much better side than they were back then. Carlo Ancelotti's men have a clear idea now of their roles and this has shown in the great results they have achieved, which have enabled them to take the lead in the race for La Liga.
    • With Messi scoring a hat-trick last weekend and Ronaldo also in great form, along with both sides being at full strength, the stage is set for a blockbuster game. There should be goals at both ends but, with Madrid the much stronger side defensively and playing in front of their fans they might just edge this one, and that could mean game-set-match for the title race.
