    Team News

    Real Madrid
    • Zinedine Zidane has confirmed he will be without Gareth Bale for the return leg, with Isco favourite to deputise in his absence. Raphael Varane and Pepe are also injury concerns coming into their second leg tie with Bayern Munich.
    Bayern Munich
    • With Mats Hummels injured and Javi Martinez suspended, David Alaba could start alongside Jerome Boateng at the heart of the defence, with Juan Bernat featuring at left-back.
    • Robert Lewandowski, who has scored five goals in five Champions League meetings with Real Madrid, is a doubt with a shoulder issue that kept him out of the first leg loss in Munich, but should be fine to start.
    • Other than that, Carlo Ancelotti will resist the temptation to tinker too much.

    Prediction

    • Real Madrid are unbeaten in eight at the Bernabeu and have lost just one competitive fixture at home all season. Los Blancos have won five of their last six home competitive meetings with Bayern and after netting twice in Munich, very much have the upper hand coming into this one.
    • Carlo Ancelotti named a much-changed XI for the weekend draw with Bayer Leverkusen and they are expected to make several changes to the side for their trip to Spain. They have, though, failed to win back-to-back away games - a rarity for Bayern - and they will be keen to end that run with a win in Madrid.
    • However, on home turf and with Bayern unable to call to key defenders here, Real Madrid should edge it.
