    Team News

    Real Madrid
    • Gareth Bale is sidelined until the end of the month with Isco expected to take his place in Real Madrid's front free. James Rodriguez and Alvaro Morata are also options.
    • Raphael Varane should partner Sergio Ramos at the heart of the back four despite being left out of the squad that beat Valencia 2-1 at the weekend.
    Atletico Madrid
    • Yannick Carrasco has recovered from a shoulder injury and may start on the right.
    • With Juanfran and Sime Vrsaljko still out and the pair joined by Jose Gimenez on the sidelines, Lucas Hernandez could start at right-back.
    • Kevin Gameiro scored two goals at the weekend and that may have earned him a starting spot ahead of Fernando Torres on Tuesday.

    Prediction

    • Real Madrid have dumped Atletico Madrid out of the Champions League in each of the last three seasons, two of which were in the final. They have only won one of their last seven away matches in the semi-finals of the Champions League so will need to take advantage of home soil on Tuesday.
    • Atletico have kept clean sheets in 18 of their last 21 home matches in the Champions League and will fancy their chances of progressing if they can score an away goal at the Bernabéu. Diego Simeone's side have won 10 and drawn six of their 22 Champions League away games since September 2013. All six defeats came by a one-goal margin.
    • No side has managed to score more than one goal in each of the last three clashes between these two teams at the Bernabéu. Expect a hard-fought encounter that could even follow the pattern of the last two meetings between the sides at the Bernabéu: draw.
