    Team News

    Wolfsburg
    • Naldo wants to play tomorrow, but is still recovering from a dislocated shoulder. Sebastian Jung has played his last match this season and won't be available.
    • Diego Benaglio made his first appearance back between the sticks in nearly two months in Friday's 3-0 defeat to Bayer Leverkusen. Maximilian Arnold also made his comeback in that match, as did Carlos Ascues.
    • Bas Dost is also closing in on a return but isn't expected to feature in the first leg.
    • Luis Gustavo is expected to come back into the starting XI after being an unused substitute in their defeat to Leverkusen on Friday.
    Real Madrid
    • Zinedine Zidane may stick with the same team that beat Barcelona in the Clasico on Saturday, which would see Isco and James Rodriguez remain on the bench.
    • Sergio Ramos was sent off against Barcelona but that ban won't carry over into European competition.
    • Raphael Varane was the only player absent from training in the build up to this fixture and has not travelled with the squad.

    Prediction

    • Real Madrid are into the quarter-finals of the Champions League for the sixth year in a row and have done so by keeping seven clean sheets from their eight European matches this season. In fact, Keylor Navas has never conceded a goal in the Champions League from his previous eight appearances (720 minutes of action) and sits behind only Jens Lehmann (853 mins) in number of minutes played without conceding in the competition.
    • Wolfsburg defeated Gent in the last round but are not expected to overcome Real Madrid over two legs here. The German side are focusing all of their resources on success in the Champions League following a dreadful league campaign, but their season could well be over after this match. Wolfsburg haven't won any of their last three and look to be suffering without Naldo at the back.
    • Madrid have won all four of their most recent two-legged ties with German sides, scoring 22 goals in the process. Zidane's side have only kept one clean sheet in their last 10 away matches in all competitions, which does give Wolfsburg some hope.
