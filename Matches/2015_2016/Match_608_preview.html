    Team News

    Barcelona
    • Gerard Pique missed the second leg of their last 16 clash with Arsenal through suspension but is free to return to the starting XI here.
    • Adriano and Jeremy Mathieu are unlikely to recover in time to face Atletico Madrid on Tuesday.
    Atletico Madrid
    • Atletico Madrid will be without Tiago and are sweating on the fitness of Gimenez. Diego Godin has been passed fit after injurying his hamstring against PSV in the last round.
    • Fernando Torres probably did enough at the weekend to earn a starting spot up front alongside Antoine Griezmann at the Camp Nou.
    • Stefan Savic was also absent from training on Monday, which could mean 20-year-old Lucas Hernandez partners Godin at the heart of their defence.

    Prediction

    • Barcelona will be looking to overcome the mental setback of defeat to Real Madrid at the weekend. That was their first defeat in all competitions since October and the first time they have been beaten on home soil February 2015. The Clasico on Saturday didn't actually have any real significance on the title race and the Barcelona players would probably have had one eye on this fixture, which is more important. Luis Enrique's side haven't lost in the Champions League since May 2013 and they have a stellar recent record against Atletico.
    • Atletico Madrid cut the gap to Barcelona to six points in La Liga at the weekend with a thumping 5-1 victory over Real Betis at the weekend. Diego Simeone will be hoping his side can carry their form in front of goal into Europe, where they haven't scored in their last 245 minutes of action.
    • Barcelona have won each of their last six matches against Atletico and are slight favourites ahead of the first-leg here. The complexion of the tie could swing firmly in Barcelona's favour if Diego Godin and Gimenez fail to recover in time.
