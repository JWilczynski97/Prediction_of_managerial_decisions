    Team News

    Man Utd
    Tottenham
    • Louis van Gaal almost has a full squad to pick from for United's Premier League opener against Spurs.
    • Marouane Fellaini will serve the first of his three-match ban after being sent off against Hull on the last day of last season.
    • Marcos Rojo is unlikely to feature having returned late to pre-season from Copa America duty and Javier Hernandez and Anders Lindegaard may also have to watch from the stands as they recover from injury.
    • Van Gaal has confirmed that David de Gea won't start on Saturday, with new signing Sergio Romero likely to feature from the off, though young Sam Johnstone is pushing for a place in the team.
    • Fitness problems could mean Bastian Schweinsteiger will make his United debut from the bench and it's a toss up between Phil Jones and Chris Smalling for whom partners Daley Blind at the heart of the defence.
    • Mauricio Pochettino's main concern will be whether or not Hugo Lloris is fit enough to play. The France international has been recovering from a wrist injury, but should be included after making it through their pre-season win over AC Milan unscathed on Wednesday.
    • Midfield trio Ryan Mason, Andros Townsend and Alex Pritchard are unlikely to make the trip, however. All three are struggling for fitness.

    Prediction

    • Louis van Gaal will be hoping to kick-off his second season in charge in better fashion than his first, where United lost to Swansea on the opening day of the season. If United want to be considered genuine title contenders they cannot afford to lose ground on their rivals at the first hurdle.
    • No player has scored more goals in this fixture than Wayne Rooney (9) and he will be hoping to kick start what could prove to be his biggest season in United colours.
    • As for Spurs, their preparation has been far from ideal. Mauricio Pochettino's men only returned home from their pre-season excursions in Germany on Thursday and you can't help but think that could play a part in Saturday's early kick-off.
    • United triumphed 3-0 the last time these two sides met and Saturday's scoreline may not look too dissimilar.
