    Team News

    Chelsea
    Swansea
    • Chelsea will start the new campaign with almost the same XI that finished the last as Premier League champions.
    • The only exception will be whether Jose Mourinho opts for Falcao or Loic Remy to deputise in the expected absence of Diego Costa, whose reoccurring hamstring problems make him a major doubt.
    • Gary Monk is able to pick from his best players ahead of their tough Premier League curtain raiser.
    • The Swansea boss could hand out debuts to summer signings Andre Ayew and Franck Tabanou at Stamford Bridge.
    • Monk has several option in midfield and could look to utilise Jack Cork and Ki Sung-yueng's defensive qualities to protect the back four, which would see Johnjo Shelvey miss out.
    • Having fought to keep Bafetimbi Gomis at the club this summer, it would be surprising to see summer signing Eder start on Saturday.
    • Swansea are optimistic Jordi Amat will have recovered from his foot injury in time to make the trip but he isn't likely to play a part.

    Prediction

    • Chelsea may not have been convincing during pre-season but it would be naive to write off their chances of retaining the Premier League title already. The Blues have drawn criticism from some quarters for their recrutiment this summer, but having won the title last season at a canter, what changes were people expecting?
    • Jose Mourinho's side produced one of the finest displays of the 2014/15 campaign when these sides last two met and Swansea will be hoping to put in a better performance this time around.
    • If travelling to Stamford Bridge to face the Premier League champions wasn't already a tall order, Gary Monk will also have to become the first manager to deny Mourinho of all three points on the opening weekend of a Premier League campaign if Swansea are going to come away with anything on Saturday.
    • Chelsea are notoriously strong starters and Mourinho is expected to keep his 100% opening weekend record here.
