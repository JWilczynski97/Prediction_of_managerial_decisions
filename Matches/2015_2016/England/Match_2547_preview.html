    Team News

    Arsenal
    Liverpool
    • Arsenal have no fresh injury concerns and are likely to start with the same XI that battled to a 2-1 victory away to Crystal Palace in their last outing.
    • Tomas Rosicky is on the right path to returning from injury, but Jack Wilshere and Danny Welbeck are still out for the next few weeks at the very least.
    • Daniel Sturridge, Joe Allen and Jon Flanagan are all sidelined for Monday's fixture and they could be joined by captain Jordan Henderson, who limped off against Bournemouth with a foot injury.
    • After Henderson picked up an injury against Bournemouth, Can and Lucas look set to move into the midfield.
    • After promising cameos against Stoke and Bournemouth, Rodgers looks set to hand Brazilian Roberto Firmino his first Premier League start.
    • Adam Lallana will miss out after being ruled out for a month with a groin injury picked up in training.

    Prediction

    • This fixture hasn't really come at a good time for either side. Liverpool may have won both of their opening league matches but their performances haven't been entirely convincing. The Liverpool players still look to be coming up to speed with what Brendan Rodgers is demanding and they will need to improve if they are to come away from Arsenal with any points.
    • As for Arsenal, they followed up their shock opening day defeat at home to West Ham with a hard-earned win at Crystal Palace. Like Liverpool, they are yet to hit top gear, but can be encouraged by the form already showed by Alexis Sanchez.
    • Arsenal ran riot in this corresponding fixture last season, but with both teams still trying to find their expected match intensity, it is unlikely there will be a repeat of the 4-1 scoreline enjoyed by the home side in April.
