    Man City

    • Arouna Kone should keep his place on the right then, meaning the likes of Naismith, Mirallas and Deulofeu will have to make do with a place on the bench once more.
    • Fernando is likely to remain sidelined for City, though summer signing Fabian Delph is nearing a return to fitness, perhaps more likely to come next week.
    • New arrival Nicolas Otamendi will have to make do with a place on the bench initially, with Mangala set to keep his place after the comprehensive win over Chelsea.

    • Indeed, Manuel Pellegrini's team is also likely to remain unchanged.

    Prediction

    • Everton shocked many with their 3-0 win over Southampton last time out, perhaps even more so than City did with their humbling of champions Chelsea, so it's important that the Toffees build on that result rather than let it be a one off.
    • The league leaders have began the campaign in scintilating form, though City have won just one of their last 6 matches at Goodison Park and always find it hard going against Everton.
    • Expect the hosts to put up more of a fight than either of the two opponents to Pellegrini's men thus far but the visitors should just come out on top.
