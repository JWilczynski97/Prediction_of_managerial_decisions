    Team News

    Arsenal
    Man City
    • With so many injury concerns the Arsenal side almost picks itself, particularly following an impressive wins over Olympiacos and Aston Villa, with confidence likely to be high.
    • As a result the team is set to remain unchanged, with fit again Theo Walcott favoured over Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain on the right.
    • Flamini and Ramsey will keep their place in midfield in the continued absence of Coquelin and Cazorla respectively, with Joel Campbell deputising for Alexis Sanchez.
    • Arsene Wenger says Sanchez has a slim chance to make the game, although he hasn't started training yet.
    • The big news is that Sergio Aguero is back and ready to go from the off, but they are still without their captain Vincent Kompany.
    • Manuel Pellegrini will have to get his team selection spot on, particularly in midfield where they have been undone several times in recent memory. Yaya Toure's position will be the main talking point, and if he is deployed in central midfield then who plays alongside him could be in for a long afternoon.
    • Fernandinho and Fabian Delph are pushing to start in central midfield. Kevin de Bruyne was rested in their win last week but is expected to return to the starting XI here. The same is true of Aleksandar Kolarov.

    Prediction

    • These used to be the fixtures where Arsenal were comprehensively turned over, but that is starting to change now as Arsene Wenger's men seem to have developed a new found sense of maturity on the pitch. Arsenal are unbeaten in their last three league meetings with City, notably beating them 2-0 in Manchester in January. They have managed to get through their recent matches without dropping points, but this will be the biggest test of their thin squad, particularly with Mathieu Flamini and Aaron Ramsey in the middle.

There are also now off field distractions for Manuel Pellegrini to deal with, amid strong reports suggesting Pep Guardiola has told Bayern Munich he will leave at the end of the season. The City boss needs to get Vincent Kompany back on the pitch and his absence could prove decisive in this match.
