    Team News

    Real Madrid
    • Cristiano Ronaldo has returned to full training after missing the first leg and is expected to return to the starting XI. Karim Benzema, however, is almost certainly ruled out, which could see Lucas Vazquez or Borja Mayoral lead the line.
    • Casemiro will also need a late fitness test. He has missed training the last two days and is a huge doubt here. Zinedine Zidane was tight-lipped when asked how he would set up his team in the absence of Casemiro, but he could revert to a 4-2-3-1 system.
    Manchester City
    • Yaya Toure has returned to training after he missed the first-leg through injury and he could deputise in the absence of the injured David Silva.
    • It remains to be seen whether Manuel Pellegrini will turn to Raheem Sterling or persist with Jesus Navas here.

    Prediction

    • A 0-0 draw was by no means a bad result for Manchester City in the home leg last week. They are still in the tie but have it all to do against a Real Madrid side that are very strong at home. In fact, Madrid haven't conceded at home in a Champions League match in nearly a year, a run that stretches back five matches.
    • Much of Real Madrid's chances hinge on whether they can recover Cristiano Ronaldo and Karim Benzema for the second leg on Wednesday. Ronaldo has scored 17 goals in the Champions League this season, only one less than City have managed as a team so far.
    • Real Madrid have only failed to win 2 of their last 20 home matches in the Champions League and they have only failed to win three of their home matches all season. This could prove a bridge too far for Pellegrini's men.
