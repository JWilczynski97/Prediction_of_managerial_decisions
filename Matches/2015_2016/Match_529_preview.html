    Team News

    Arsenal
    • Olivier Giroud is available having missed their last Champions League outing through suspension, although he isn't expected to usurp Theo Walcott up front.
    • Gabriel is available again after missing their league win over Watford at the weekend.
    • Jack Wilshere, Danny Welbeck, Tomas Rosicky, Mathieu Flamini and Mikel Arteta are all still out injured. David Ospina is also out injured which means Petr Cech will start his first Champions League game for Arsenal.
    Bayern Munich
    • Arjen Robben didn't travel with the squad and will not feature here, but Robert Lewandowski has been passed fit to play. Pep Guardiola will still be without Mario Gotze, who was injured during the international break and it is still too soon for the likes of Franck Ribery and Mehdi Benatia.
    • Guardiola has used a 4-3-3 formation in both of his first Champions League fixtures this season and could revert to that here.
    • Javi Martinez could partner Jerome Boateng at the heart of Bayern's defence with David Alaba moving back to the left hand side. Douglas Costa is set to have a fitness test prior to the match and could be replaced by Kingsley Coman if he fails to make the grade.

    Prediction

    • Arsenal have lost their opening two group matches and are now realistically looking to finish in second place. Arsene Wenger's side can still qualify if they lose both of their next to matches to Bayern Munich but it will won't be ideal to have 0 points after four matches.
    • Bayern are already favourites to finish first and are now given the luxury of being able to rest the likes of Arjen Robben for more pressing fixtures.
    • Arsenal haven't beaten Bayern at home since 2005 when Thierry Henry was the difference in a 1-0 victory. The Gunners have also only managed one goal in their last two home meetings with Bayern, conceding five.
    • Alexis Sanchez's form will be key to any victory for Arsenal. The Chile international has scored seven goals in his last four games in all competitions for Arsenal.
    • Robert Lewandowski, on the other hand, drew a blank at the weekend, bringing close to a run of 12 goals in four games for Bayern.
