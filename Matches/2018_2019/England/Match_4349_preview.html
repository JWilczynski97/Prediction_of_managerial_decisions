    Prediction

    • Manchester City moved back to the top of the Premier League table on Wednesday night as they overcame Everton 2-0, however they may start this clash in second depending on the outcome of Liverpool's welcome of Bournemouth on Saturday. That being said, City are only top on goal difference, while the Reds have a game in hand on their title contenders.
    • Chelsea breezed past Huddersfield last weekend, but they have lost their last three away games in all competitions, succumbing to defeat at Tottenham, Arsenal and Bournemouth since the turn of the year. They've also won only one of their last five visits to the Etihad Stadium, though won the reverse fixture 2-0 as goals from N'Golo Kante and David Luiz ruined City's chances of an unbeaten season.
    • City look set to add to Chelsea's recent away woes and exact revenge on the 2-0 loss at Stamford Bridge earlier in the season. The result should move the champions back to the top of the table, unless Liverpool can make up a seven-goal difference between the pair this weekend.
