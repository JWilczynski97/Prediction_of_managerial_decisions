    Team News

    Man City
    Liverpool
    • Pep Guardiola insists Kevin De Bruyne's latest injury isn't serious but it may keep the Manchester City star out of Thursday's must-win match against table-toppers Liverpool.
    • With Fabian Delph suspended and Ilkay Gndogan also an injury concern for the home side, Guardiola could be short of options in central midfield.
    • Fernandinho at least returned from injury in Sunday's 3-1 win over Southampton, which is a huge boost for the defending Premier League champions.
    • Leroy Sane was only a substitute against Southampton at the weekend and should be restored to the starting XI against Liverpool.
    • John Stones wasn an unused substitute at the weekend but will be back at the heart of City's defence. With Delph and Mendy unavailable to play at left-back, Guardiola may shift Aymeric Laporte out to left-back after Oleksandr Zinchenko's difficult afternoon against Southampton.
    • Kyle Walker was also benched against Southampton, though there are no guarentees he will return here after a dip in form.
    • Liverpool will have had ample time to recover for Thursday's clash against Manchester City after playing 24 hours earlier than their opponents over the weekend.
    • Jurgen Klopp has changed between a 4-2-3-1 and 4-3-3 formation this season, with the former allowing Xherdan Shaqiri to play, as was the case in their 5-1 victory over Arsenal on Saturday.
    • Jordan Henderson will hope to return to the side after being dropped against the Gunners, though there is serious competition in midfield.
    • James Milner returned to training in midweek and is in contention to make Liverpool's matchday squad on Thursday.

    Prediction

    • Manchester City trail Liverpool by seven points heading into Thursday's clash and anything other than a victory would make it very difficult for them to catch the Reds in the title race.
    • Jurgen Klopp is one of the few managers that seems to have got the better of Pep Guardiola more often than not, illustrated by the fact Liverpool are unbeaten in their last four competitive matches against City.
    • It's hard to see City limiting the amount of chances Liverpool will create without a recognised left-back available, coupled with a lack of depth in central midfield at the moment.
    • In the past you could have banked on outscoring Liverpool, but that has changed this season now Klopp's side have the best defensive record in the Premier League (eight goals conceded). They should take a huge step towards winning the title on Thursday.
