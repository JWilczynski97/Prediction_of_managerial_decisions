    Team News

    Manchester City
    • Sergio Aguero returned from his knee injury at the end of Saturday's Manchester derby defeat to Manchester United and should start against Liverpool here.
    • Kevin De Bruyne and Kyle Walker will also return to the starting XI after being givent he night off against United.
    • It remains to be seen whether Vincent Kompany can start three games in the space of a week, meaning John Stones could earn a recall to the starting XI on Tuesday if he can overcome his slight injury.
    Liverpool
    • With Emre Can and Jordan Henderson both unavailable for Manchester City, Liverpool have a problem at the heart of their midfield. Georginio Wijnaldum played there against Everton and may keep his position as a result.
    • Mohamed Salah was not risked in the Merseyside derby after picking up a slight groin problem against City last week but is expected to recover in time to feature here.
    • Andrew Robertson, Trent Alexander-Arnold, Roberto Firmino and Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain will also return to the starting XI after being rested on Saturday.

    Prediction

    • Are the wheels coming off Manchester City's season at the worst possible moment? They have lost successive matches for the first time under Pep Guardiola and conceded three goals in each match. They need to score at least three goals here to have any chance of staying in Europe.
    • Liverpool have one foot in the semi finals of the Champions League after beating City 3-0 at home in the first leg. They will know that one goal should be enough to secure their passage into the last four of the Champions League for the first time since the 2007/08 campaign.
    • If City can perform to the same level as they did in the first half of the Manchester derby, and take their opportunities, then they have a chance of pulling off a remarkable comeback. However, Liverpool will more than fancy their chances of doing enough to get through if City's second half performance against United was anything to go by.
