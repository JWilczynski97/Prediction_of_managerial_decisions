    Team News

    Roma
    • Kostas Manolas and Alessandro Florenzi will return to the starting XI after being given the weekend off.
    • Roma's high line was exploited to epic proportions in the first leg and Eusebio Di Francesco may revert to a back-four on Wednesday.
    • Diego Perotti and Kevin Strootman miss out for the home side and are expected to be replaced in the starting XI by Stephan El Shaarawy and Lorenzo Pellegrini, respectively.
    • Rick Karsdorp and Gregoire Defrel are also injury concerns for the home side.
    Liverpool
    • Jurgen Klopp shuffled his pack against Stoke on Saturday and will restore his usual XI in Rome on Wednesday.
    • Trent Alexander-Arnold and captain Jordan Henderson both picked up knocks at the weekend but are expected to recover in time to face Rome, but Joe Gomez has failed to recover from an ankle injury in time.
    • Alex Oxlade-Chamberlain suffered a season-ending injury in the first leg and is expected to be replaced in midfield by Georginio Wijnaldum.
    • A hamstring injury kept Sadio Mane out at the weekend but Klopp says he will be fine to play in midweek.
    • Adam Lallana has travelled with the Liverpool squad but will have to settle for a place on the bench.
    • No other changes are expected from the side that thrashed Roma 5-2 in the first leg.

    Prediction

    • Roma are chasing a 5-2 deficit from the first leg but will draw on their experience from the last round where they beat Barcelona 3-0 in the second leg to advance to the semi-finals on the away goal rule. Can lightning strike twice here?
    • Liverpool are the clear favourites and have already shown in the knockout phase of the competition they can not only protect first leg leads, but build on them. After beating Manchester City 3-0 in the first leg of their quarter-final clash, they travelled to the Etihad and won 2-1.
    • Jurgen Klopp's side were left kicking themselves for even giving Roma a chance in the second leg after conceding two late goals at Anfield, but they have more than enough firepower to prevent Roma pulling off another Champions League upset this season.
