    Prediction

    • Tottenham come into this one having secured a 2-0 win over Newport to book a place in the next round of the FA Cup and attention turns to Saturday's early kick off. The hosts are unbeaten in their last 11 competitive matches, winning seven, and are unbeaten in their last three at home against Arsenal in the league, winning two.
    • Arsenal defeated Tottenham 2-0 when these two met in November at the Emirates Stadium and while on home turf, the Gunners are solid, away from home, they've struggled. Arsene Wenger's side have won only one of their last eight competitive games on the road, with their away league form leaving little to be desired.
    • Mauricio Pochettino will hope fatigue from midweek doesn't set in, but having rested the core of his squad, that won't be the case on Saturday. Arsenal will be keen to improve their away form, but Tottenham could see out the three points in what should be an entertaining encounter at Wembley.
