    Prediction

    • This is the game everyone has been waiting for, El Clasico. Both teams could hardly be in better form either. Real Madrid enter this game on the back of five consecutive wins in La Liga and have been scoring goals for fun, thanks to Cristiano Ronaldo's ridiculously good form. Barcelona, likewise, have been conquering all before them and are still yet to concede a goal.
    • As always, much of this match centres around the battle between two sensational footballers - Ronaldo and Lionel Messi. Ronaldo leads his rival in goals, with 15 in seven matches. Still, the Argentine could steal the limelight if he scores a brace as it will see him break Telmo Zarra's record of 251 for goals scored in La Liga - a record that has stood since 1955. And Messi does tend to score against Real Madrid - he already has 21 goals against Los Blancos in his career.
    • Key to Real Madrid's chances in this game will be whether Sergio Ramos plays or not. The Spain international picked up an injury two weeks ago and only returned to training this week. Carlo Ancelotti will be hoping he can prove his fitness in time.
    • Another major storyline in this game is the return of Luis Suarez. Banned by Fifa since he decided to take a chunk of out Giorgio Chiellini's shoulder in the World Cup, the Uruguayan has yet to make his debut for his club. And should he, it will make for a terrifyingly potent front line alongside Neymar and Lionel Messi.
    • Spain's bookmakers are tipping Real Madrid, but only just. Though the Catalans lead the way in the table, the hosts have been in better form, scoring goals and playing attractive football. All seems very well at Casa Blanca, but if any side can spoil the positive vibe, it is Barcelona and, likewise, if any one can, it is Lionel Messi.
