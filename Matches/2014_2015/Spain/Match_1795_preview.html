    Prediction

    • Despite some early season wobbles, Carlo Ancelotti deserves endless praise for finding the right balance in a side littered with so many wonderful talents. This weekend one of the best, Gareth Bale, returns to the fold. The Welshman has been out for three weeks and with the side having played so well without him, Madrid's more patriotic fans were calling for the young Spain international, Isco, to keep his place. But it is expected that Bale will start with Isco dropping to the bench.
    • Real Madrid are almost averaging four goals per game in the league this season - an incredible statistic. In their last seven games, they have scored 32 goals, an average of 4.5 goals per game. Such is their form that some in Spain are comparing them with the great Barca side under Pep Guardiola. While they still have a lot in order to emulate Pep's all-conquering side, they definitely deserve such praise.
    • Rayo's open style of play means we will see a Madrid side focusing more on the counter-attack rather than keeping possession. This is especially likely with Gareth Bale expected to be included in the starting XI.
