    Team News

    Bayern Munich
    • Pep Guardiola has numerous injury concerns to deal with ahead of the welcome of Barcelona.
    • The Bayern boss is without injured sextet David Alaba, Holger Badstuber, Franck Ribery, Arjen Robben, Sebastian Rode and Tom Starke.
    • Robert Lewandowski is a doubt with a facial injury, but is expected to don a protective mask once more, with Guardiola perhaps switching formation to counter the Barcelona attack.
    Barcelona
    • Luis Enrique has no injury or suspension concerns coming into this one, with the Barcelona head coach able to field his strongest XI.
    • Marc-Andre ter Stegen, as is the custom in cup games, will start in goal in place of Claudio Bravo.

    Prediction

    • Bayern succumbed to a 3-0 defeat in Barcelona last week and they need a huge turnaround to progress to the final of the Champions League. Recent performances have left a lot to be desired, however - the Bavarian powerhouse have lost their last 4 competitive games, netting just once in the process.
    • Barcelona, meanwhile, all but secured the La Liga title at the weekend following victory Real Sociedad and results going their way. The Blaugrana hold all the cards coming into this encounter, though Luis Enrique will warn against complacency as the Spanish side aim to secure a final berth.
    • With Barcelonas front three in form and Bayern struggling in defence and attack, the Spanish outfit should secure victory at the Allianz.
