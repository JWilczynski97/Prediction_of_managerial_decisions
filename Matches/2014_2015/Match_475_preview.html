    Team News

    Real Madrid
    • James Rodriguez is sidelined with a metatarsal injury and misses out for Los Blancos.
    • Sergio Ramos is closing in on a return to fitness, while manager Carlo Ancelotti has confirmed that Luka Modric will start on the bench on Tuesday night.
    • Ancelotti may be tempted to name the same XI that started against Athletic over the weekend.
    Schalke 04
    • Roberto di Matteo will be without a number of key players through injury when he takes his side to Madrid on Tuesday night.
    • Julian Draxler, Ralf Fahrmann, Jefferson Farfan and Chinedu Obasi are all sidelined. Joel Matip, Sead Kolasinac, Fabian Giefer and Jan Kirchhoff are all classed as doubts.
    • Kevin-Prince Boateng, meanwhile, is suspended, although Di Matteo has one eye on league duties and is likely to leave the likes of Benedikt Howedes, Marco Hoger and Klaas-Jan Huntelaar out of the starting XI.

    Prediction

    • Real Madrid hold the upper hand coming into the second leg following their 2-0 win over Schalke in Germany last month. However, Los Blancos have dropped points in their last 2 league meetings, including a dissappointing 1-0 loss at Athletic Club on Saturday. Carlo Ancelotti will demand his players return to winning ways here.
    • Schalke's 3-1 win over Hoffenheim ended a 4-game winless run, which will have boosted morale ahead of the trip to the Bernabeu. Nevertheless, the German side have conceded 11 goals in their last 3 meetings with Real Madrid and their poor form against the Primera Division giants is unlikely to change in this one, especially with Di Matteo having taken a weakened team to Spain.
    • While Madrid have underwhelmed of late, they have what it takes to secure a comfortable victory.
