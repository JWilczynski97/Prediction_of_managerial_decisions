    Team News

    Bayern Munich
    • Bayern have plenty of injury concerns for this second leg, in which they need to win by at least two goals and will field a typically attacking team.
    • Philipp Lahm and Juan Bernat are both doubtful, and whether they are fit will affect the starting lineup.
    • There is a chance that Franck Ribery could return, and presumably he will play a part if he is able to.
    FC Porto
    • Porto will be without both of their first choice full-backs for the trip to Munich.
    • Danilo and Alex Sandro are suspended after picking up yellow cards in the first leg. They could be replaced by Ricardo Pereira and Marcano here.
    • Jackson Martinez is a doubt with a groin injury, but he should come through.

    Prediction

    • Porto go to the Allianz with a 3-1 lead to protect in this quarter-final second leg clash. Playing away at Bayern is never an easy task, but going there with the mindset of needing to hold out for a draw - or 1-goal defeat - may not be the right way to go about this game.
    • They might be better off playing a similar style to last week and looking to press their opponents into yet more uncharacteristic mistakes.
    • Bayern will attack from the off and will be likely to win this clash. The fact that the bookies still have them at less than evens to qualify speaks volumes about the task that faces Porto, and it wouldn't be all that surprising if the Germans were to overturn the deficit and progress.
